Introduction

Hi! I'm Joey, a third-year Electrical Engineering student at McMaster University. I'm interested in embedded systems, circuit design, and the intersection of hardware and software. I enjoy building and testing hardware prototypes, writing firmware that runs efficiently, and creating practical solutions that actually work outside the lab.

My experience spans programming with C, C++, and Python for embedded systems and microcontroller development, as well as circuit analysis and simulation using PSpice and LTspice. On the hardware side, I've worked extensively with platforms like the MSP-EXP432E401Y microcontroller, Arduino/UNO R3, and the Analog Discovery 3 for measurement and debugging. These projects have strengthened my ability to design and prototype reliable circuits, write efficient firmware, and integrate hardware and software into complete, tested systems.
